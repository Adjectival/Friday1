About me

I served as a 'cryptographic IT guy' in the US Air Force for awhile, and meeting those challenges taught me to enjoy troubleshooting technical problems. This experience, plus years spent tinkering with computers and software, eventually lead me to the vocational programming school where I'm learning to write production-grade code full time.

After moving to Oregon (and before deciding to learn how to program) I studied neuroscience and earned a BS in Psychology at Portland State University. Currently I'm fascinated by the web technologies I learn and use daily, and the expanding arena of cloud services. I live in Southeast Portland with:

  • my wife (a pop culture savant and medical Data Analyst)
  • our aquarium of fish and snails
  • our kombucha yeasts & microbes
    • sometimes less exotic yeasts, if I'm homebrewing beer

I helped move Top Secret data securely in the USAF, and now I like to write code.

Beer occurs when certain yeasts find a sugary fluid to eat and then they make alcohol. Portland has more breweries than any other city (so the hipsters claim).
